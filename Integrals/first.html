Integral of $ \int_0^{\infty} \frac{\cos(x)}{1 + x^2} \mathrm{d}x $

We'll solve it using Laplace Transform,

let $$ \mathcal{I} = \int_0^{\infty} \frac{\cos(x)}{1 + x^2} \mathrm{d}x $$ And we know that Laplace Transform of $f(t)$ is $$ \mathcal{L}(f(t)) = \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} f(t) \mathrm{d}t $$ Now let
$$ \mathcal{I}(\alpha) = \int_0^{\infty} \frac{\cos(\alpha x)}{1 + x^2} \mathrm{d}x $$ $$ \implies \mathcal{I}(1) = \mathcal{I} $$
\begin{align} \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{I}(\alpha)) &= \int_0^\infty e^{-s\alpha} \int_0^{\infty} \frac{\cos(\alpha x)}{1 + x^2} \mathrm{d}x \hspace{1mm} \mathrm{d}\alpha \\ &= \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{1 + x^2} \int_0^{\infty} e^{-s\alpha} \cos(\alpha x) \mathrm{d}\alpha \hspace{1mm} \mathrm{d}x \\ &= \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{1 + x^2} \left( \frac{s}{s^2 + x^2} \right) \hspace{1mm} \mathrm{d}x \\ &= \frac{s}{s^2 - 1} \int_0^\infty \left( \frac{1}{1 + x^2} - \frac{1}{s^2 + x^2} \right) \hspace{1mm} \mathrm{d}x \\ &= \frac{s}{s^2 - 1} \left( \tan^{-1}(x) - \frac{1}{s} \tan^{-1}(x/s) \right) \Bigg |_0^\infty \\ &= \frac{s}{s^2 - 1} \left( \frac{\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{2s} \right) \\ &= \frac{\pi}{2(s + 1)} \\ \end{align}
Now taking inverse Laplace Transform
$$ \mathcal{I}(\alpha) = \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left( \frac{\pi}{2(s + 1)} \right) $$ $$ \mathcal{I}(\alpha) = \frac{\pi}{2} e^{-\alpha} $$
