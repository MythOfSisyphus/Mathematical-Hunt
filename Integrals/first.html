Integral of $ \int_0^{\infty} \frac{\cos(x)}{1 + x^2} \mathrm{d}x $

We'll solve it using Laplace Transform, so

let $$ \mathcal{I} = \int_0^{\infty} \frac{\cos(x)}{1 + x^2} \mathrm{d}x $$ And we know that Laplace Transform of $f(t)$ is $$ \mathcal{L}(f(t)) = \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} f(t) \mathrm{d}t $$ Now let $$ \mathcal{I}(\alpha) = \int_0^{\infty} \frac{\cos(\alpha x)}{1 + x^2} \mathrm{d}x , \hspace{10mm} \mathcal{I}(1) = \mathcal{I} $$
